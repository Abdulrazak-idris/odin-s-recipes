NIGERIAN SPAGETTI

DESCRIPTION

We Nigerians love our jollof rice!! Jollof Spaggetti is an adaption of this populer dish, Using Spagetti instead of rice. The inclution of sardines, and additional Vegetables makes it a complete meal. If you are looking for new ways to eat pasta, you must try Nigerian jollof spagetti with sardines. It is easy to make, and always require staple pantry ingredients. This delicious Nigerian-Style pasta comes together quickly, is cheap to make, nutritious and packed full of flavours.

Jollof Spagetti is a delicious alternative to everyday Nigerian Jollof Rice. This recipe is incredibly delicious and cook way faster than the rice version. perfect for lunch or dinner. You can also serve this for guest for a change. A simple dish of Spaggetti and tomateo sauce, in this case (buka-stew) and other pantry staple ingredients you may already have in your store cupboard.

INGREDIENTS

  • Spagetti.
  • Buka-Stew.
  • Tomatoe puree.
  • Nigerian pepper mix.
  • Chilli flakes.
  • Salt.
  • Vegetable oil.
  • Curry powder.
  • Dried thyme.
  • Uziza leaves.
  • Tomatoes, Sliced (optional).
  • Onions.
  • Dried basil (optional).
  • Pasta water.
  • Fresh Prawns (optional).

COOKING-STEPS

  1. PREP!!
    • chilli flakes to puree and mixx together. cook the Spaggetti in salted boiling water until al-dente.
  2. COOK!!
    • >Place a pan on medium heat, add vegetable oil and chopped onions. Fry till onions becomes translucent. This should take about two to three minutes.
  3. Add a left-over stew, and cook for about 3 minutes, followed by the Tomato puree mixture, curry powder, thyme, salt and Chicken bouillon (add water from spagetti) to dilute the sauce about 1 cup.
