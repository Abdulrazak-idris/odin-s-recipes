NIGERIAN FRIED RICE

DESCRIPTION

Fried rice is a dish of cooked rice that been stir-fried in a wok or frying pan, and is usually mixed with other ingredients such as EGGS, VEGETABLES, SEAFOOD, or MEAT. It is often eaten by itself, or as an accompaniment to another dish.

Fried Rice is a classic chinese dish that often appears on restaurant menus. This delightful, delicious jumble of rice and mix-ins also boast along. Illustration history that cannot be ignored.

COOKING-STEPS

  1. Rinse the rice with cold water, pour it into a large pot.
    Place 1 cup(178g) of dry rice into a fine mesh strainer. Run cold water over the rice and use your clean hands to gently rinse the rice.
  2. Add two (2) cups of water to the large pot.
    When parboiling, use twice as much water as you do rice. In this case, use 2 cups (473ml) of water since you are parboiling 1 cup (178g) of dry rice.
  3. Boil the mixture over high heat for five minutes.
    Bring the water to a full rolling boil. Then start your timer and let the rice boiled for five minutes. Keep the lid off the pot while the liquid heats up, so it doesn't boil over.
  4. Transfer the rice to a fine mesh strainer.
    Turn off the beat and hold a fine mesh strainer over your sink. Pour the mixture into the strainer to drain the water from the rice.
  5. Rinse off the rice with cold water.
    Take the strainer out of the bowl of water, and rinse off the rice again with cold water from the faucet. At this point, your rice is parboiled and ready to use in this recipe.
  6. Put 1/2 cup (118ml) of stock in a large pot and bring it to a boil.
    Nigerian Fried Rice is traditionally made with Chicken or turkey stock. Add 1/2 cup (118ml) of the stock to a large pot or saucepan. Let the liqiud come to a full rolling boil.
    • In traditional Nigerian recipe, rice is always cooked on meat stock (unlike the rice in Chinese Fried rice, Which is cooked in plain water.)
    • If you prefer, use beef or vegetable stock instead.
  7. Add the parboiled rice and boil the mixture for 25 minutes.
    Carefully put the parboiled rice into the boiling stock. Boil the rice until it absorbs all of the stock, which usually takes around 25 minutes.
